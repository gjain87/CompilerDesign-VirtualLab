Theory -

In computer programming, an operator is a symbol that tells the compiler to perform specific mathematical or logical functions.

Procedure -

There are different types of operators in a programming language to perform fundamental and logical operations. They are classified as follows -

  • Arithmetic Operators   [+ , - , * , / , %  ]
  • Relational Operators   [ == ,  != >,  < , >= ,  <= ]
  • Logical Operators   [ && , || , ! ]
  • Bitwise Operators   [ & , | , ^ ]

Therefore we need to check the input string for every possible combination of operators and then categories it under the different categories.
